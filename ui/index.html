Introducing Structured Context Protocol: A New Standard for LLM-Ready Documents

We're releasing SCP 1.0, an open specification for encoding web documents in a format natively understood by large language models. SCP solves the problem of context fragmentation where long pages lose coherence when chunked for retrieval.

Initiatives

Programs shaping the future of AI-readable content

01
Open llms.txt Standard

A community-driven specification for the llms.txt format, making it easy for site owners to publish machine-readable summaries of their content without a crawl pipeline.

02
Economic Futures

Studying how AI-powered content discovery reshapes information economics, from publisher monetisation to search engine dynamics and the long-term value of structured web data.

03
Model Transparency Initiative

Publishing our model cards, data provenance, and evaluation benchmarks so developers can make informed decisions about which AI extraction pipeline fits their use case.

04
Responsible Crawling Coalition

Working with site owners, search engines, and standards bodies to establish ethical norms for AI crawlers: consent signals, rate-limit headers, and opt-out registries.
